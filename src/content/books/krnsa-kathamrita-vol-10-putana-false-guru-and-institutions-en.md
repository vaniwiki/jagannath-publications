oi namani pranayena te sukrtinam tanvanti tundotsavam
dhamani prathayanti hanta jalada-syamani netranjanam
samani sruti-saskulim muralika-jatany alankurvate
kamanirvrta-cetasam iha vibho nasapi nah sobhate

Now that we have become your devotees, your holy names have affectionately
created a jubilant festival in our mouths, your bodily splendor, which is like a
dark raincloud, has become the black ointment of our eyes, and the music
of your flute has become the ornament of our ears. O Almighty Lord, we no longer
find happiness in so-called pleasurable objects, and material desires have lost their
attractiveness. — Srila Rupa Goswami’s Padyavali, text 59
